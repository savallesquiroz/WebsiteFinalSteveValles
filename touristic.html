Royal Palace

The Royal Palace of Madrid is a grand 18th-century palace located in the heart of the city, and is the official residence of the Spanish Royal Family. It features over 3,400 rooms, lavishly decorated with priceless art, furniture, and artifacts. The palace is a popular tourist destination, offering visitors a glimpse into the opulent lifestyle of Spanish royalty and the country's rich cultural heritage.

Prado Museum

The Prado Museum is one of the world's most renowned art museums, located in Madrid, Spain. It houses an extensive collection of over 35,000 masterpieces from artists such as Francisco de Goya, Diego Velázquez, and El Greco, among others. The collection spans from the 12th to the 19th centuries, and includes some of the most famous works of art in the world, including Las Meninas by Velázquez and The Garden of Earthly Delights by Hieronymus Bosch. The Prado Museum is a must-see attraction for art lovers and history buffs visiting Madrid.

Retiro Park

Retiro Park is a large public park located in the heart of Madrid, Spain. It was originally a royal park, and features beautiful gardens, fountains, sculptures, and a large artificial lake where visitors can rent boats. The park also houses a number of historic buildings, including the Crystal Palace, which is now used as an exhibition space for contemporary art. Retiro Park is a popular destination for both tourists and locals, offering a tranquil escape from the bustling city streets.

Santiago Bernabéu

The Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is a world-renowned football stadium located in Madrid, Spain. It is the home stadium of Real Madrid, one of the most successful football clubs in the world. The stadium has a seating capacity of over 80,000 spectators and features state-of-the-art facilities, including a museum that showcases the history of the club and its legendary players. The Santiago Bernabéu Stadium is a must-visit destination for football fans visiting Madrid.
